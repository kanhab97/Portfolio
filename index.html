Identifying different types of cell nuclei in cancer samples using ConvNets

In this Project, I successfully trained two deep neural networks to classify cell nuclei in digital pathology images of colon tissue. The goal was to distinguish between different cell types, including normal epithelial cell nuclei, cancer epithelial cell nuclei, muscle cell nuclei, and immune leukocyte cell nuclei. To accomplish this, I utilized a dataset consisting of 100x100 pixel images with cell nuclei positioned at the center. The images were preprocessed and segmented to isolate the nuclei regions of interest. Then, I employed deep neural networks, specifically convolutional neural networks (CNNs), to learn and extract meaningful features from the nuclei images.

The first deep neural network was trained to classify the nuclei into the four specified cell types. I utilized appropriate loss functions, such as categorical cross-entropy, and employed optimization techniques like stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to train the model. Through multiple iterations of training, validation, and fine-tuning, the model achieved satisfactory accuracy and performance metrics.

The second deep neural network focused specifically on identifying cancer epithelial cell nuclei, distinguishing them from the other cell types. This network was designed to enhance sensitivity towards cancerous nuclei, as the accurate identification of malignant cells is crucial in diagnosing colon cancer.

Throughout the project, I implemented various data augmentation techniques to increase the robustness and generalization of the models. These techniques included image rotations, flips, and zooms, which helped prevent overfitting and improve the models' ability to classify unseen nuclei accurately.

The trained models exhibited promising results, demonstrating their ability to accurately classify cell nuclei in colon tissue images. By incorporating machine learning and digital pathology, this approach has the potential to aid pathologists in diagnosing colon cancer more efficiently and accurately.
